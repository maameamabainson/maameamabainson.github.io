Maame Ama Bainson

I am currently pursuing my PhD in Mathematical Modelling, Analysis, and Computation at the Maxwell Institute for Mathematical Sciences, a joint centre between the University of Edinburgh and Heriot-Watt University.

My current research focuses on stochastic modelling of disease dynamics, using WKB approximation and Hamiltonian systems to estimate mean persistence times of endemic diseases under Prof. Damian Clancy.

Prior to this, I earned an MSc in Mathematical Sciences with distinction from the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS Ghana), where my thesis explored forward-backward stochastic differential equations with jumps.

Research

My research lies at the intersection of stochastic modelling, mathematical biology, and dynamical systems, with a focus on understanding and designing eradication policies for endemic diseases through probabilistic and analytical tools.

A central theme of my work is understanding how rare events—such as disease extinction—can be predicted and quantified in complex, noisy systems. I am especially interested in how advanced mathematical methods, like WKB approximation and Hamiltonian systems, can inform real-world public health strategies. Some research questions I am currently exploring include:

  • How can we use stochastic analysis to estimate the persistence time of endemic diseases in a population?
  • What insights can Hamiltonian structures provide about transitions in noisy epidemiological systems?
  • How can mathematical modelling inform the design of optimal eradication strategies under uncertainty?
